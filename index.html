Research Profile

Expertise in Aerosol Science & Environmental Engineering

I am a PhD researcher at Indian Institute of Technology Gandhinagar specializing in Aerosol Science and its applications in environmental engineering. My research focuses on air pollution monitoring, spatio-temporal modeling, and the design of low-cost IoT sensor networks for real-time air quality assessment.

My core expertise lies in developing advanced machine learning and deep learning models (CNN, GNN, LSTM) for PM₂.₅ prediction and air quality forecasting. I integrate high-resolution air quality data with meteorological features to generate impactful, research-driven insights that support environmental decision-making and public health policy.

Research Domains

Core Research Focus Areas

🌫️ Aerosol Science

Understanding aerosol formation, transport, and transformation in atmospheric environments. Focus on particle size distribution, composition analysis, and health implications.

💨 Air Quality Monitoring

Real-time ambient and indoor air pollution assessment using multi-pollutant networks (PM₂.₅, PM₁₀, NO₂, O₃, TVOCs). Health risk quantification and exposure analysis.

📡 IoT Sensor Networks

Design, deployment, and calibration of low-cost sensor networks for distributed air quality monitoring. Data fusion and quality assurance protocols.

🤖 ML/AI for AQ Prediction

Advanced machine learning (CNN, GNN, LSTM) and deep learning models for PM₂.₅ forecasting and spatio-temporal air quality dynamics using meteorological and atmospheric features.
