Potential Health Risk Estimation Through Deterministic and Stochastic Approaches for the Air Pollutants Found Inside Bottling Beverage Industries of Himachal Pradesh in India

Health Risk Assessment Indoor Air Quality Exposure Analysis

Indramani Dhada, Prince Vats, Shailesh Kumar Samal

Published in: Discover Environment, Springer, March 2025

  • Assessed indoor air pollution in 12 bottling and beverage industries during the monsoon season.
  • Monitored TVOCs, PM10, PM2.5, PM1, black carbon (BC), and noise levels.
  • Performed deterministic and probabilistic health risk assessments, identifying non-carcinogenic hazard ratios (HRs) between 0.52–0.80.
  • Determined highest risk in males aged 50–60 and females aged 40–50.
  • Estimated carcinogenic risk of BC exposure between 5.8E-04 and 6.8E-04.
  • Recommended ventilation improvements and targeted mitigation strategies.

Evaluating CNN-LSTM and GNN-GRU Hybrid Models for Enhanced Air Quality Prediction and Dynamic Forecasting

Machine Learning Air Quality Prediction Deep Learning
  • Compared deep learning models for 24-hour PM2.5 prediction: CNN-LSTM vs. GNN-GRU.
  • Used hourly PM2.5 data from 35 stations in Delhi. Achieved RMSE of 26.67 μg/m³ with optimized CNN-LSTM.
  • Demonstrated improved spatial accuracy using GNN-GRU with reduced computational load.
  • Applied feature engineering and PCA to enhance interpretability and performance.

Evaluating Multiple ML Models for AQI Prediction in Gangetic Plain Using Air Quality and Meteorological Features

Target Journal: Environmental Monitoring and Assessment

Machine Learning AQI Forecasting Ensemble Methods
  • Evaluated 15 ML models for PM2.5-based AQI prediction in 25 Gangetic Plain cities using air quality and meteorological data (2018–2023).
  • Extra Trees was the top performer, then Gradient Boosting, LightGBM, and XGBoost.
  • Linear models and SVR showed higher errors, especially in high-variability cities like Lucknow and Muzaffarpur, due to inability to capture nonlinear AQI-meteorology links.
  • Ensemble models consistently gave lower errors, stronger generalization, and stable residuals. Findings highlight ensemble ML's superiority for accurate, city-specific AQI forecasting.
